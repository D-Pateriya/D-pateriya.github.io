Research Profile

Deepika has a rich academic background in analyzing human gut microbiome datasets to uncover critical health biomarkers and bacterial dynamics.

Her expertise encompasses human microbiome bioinformatics, large-scale metagenomic data analysis, and machine learning-based disease prediction. She is highly experienced in analyzing complex datasets to characterize microbial community structure and functional diversity, which directly feeds into biomarker discovery.

During her PhD at IISER Bhopal, she developed robust computational pipelines, integrated multi-omics datasets, and performed population-level comparative analyses. She remains deeply interested in investigating host-microbe interactions, microbial metabolism, and microbial community ecology.
